"Guide to Get a Battle Royale Win"

Tor Jones

Things I like: Getting Dubs, Playing with Friends, and Teaching Others


Traits of a Professional Battle Royalist

  1. Maintains sharp situational awareness to track enemies, resources, and safe zones.

  2. Demonstrates strong decision-making under pressure during fast-paced encounters.

  3. Shows consistent accuracy, precision, and control with a variety of weapons.

  4. Communicates effectively with teammates to coordinate strategies and movements.

  5. Adapts quickly to changing conditions such as shrinking zones or shifting opponents.

  6. Practices consistent movement techniques to avoid becoming an easy target.

  7. Manages inventory efficiently, balancing healing items, shields, and ammunition.

  8. Applies tactical planning, including positioning, rotations, and map awareness.

  9. Stays calm during close-range engagements and clutch scenarios.

  10. Shows patience and discipline, choosing the right moments to fight or disengage.

1. Choose the right drop spot

Your early decisions matter a lot.

  • Avoid highly contested hot drops unless you’re very confident.
  • Choose areas with:
    • Good loot
    • Fewer players
    • Rotations to safe zones
  • Learn 3–4 reliable landing spots so you always know where the loot and cover are.

2. Prioritize Smart Looting

Once you land:

  • Grab a weapon first, even if it’s low tier.
  • Always carry:
    • Close-range weapon (SMG/shotgun)
    • Mid-range rifle
    • Healing (shields + health)
    • Utility (grenades, scans, traps, mobility)
  • Don’t waste time looting too long—rotate before the zone forces you.

3. Play for Position, Not Kills

  • Stay in zone, rotate early.
  • Avoid crossing open areas last-minute—move with cover.
  • Hold high ground, ridges, buildings, or natural cover.
  • If third parties are common, wait 2–3 seconds before entering fights.

4. Master the Art of Fighting

Aim & Shooting

  • Aim for the upper body + head.
  • Strafe left/right unpredictably.
  • Commit to fights only if:
    • You have cover
    • You have armor advantage
    • You start the fight by dealing damage first

Positioning

  • Don’t stand still.
  • Don’t fight multiple squads at once.
  • Use height, doors, windows, and corners to win trades.

Utilities

  • Grenades force enemies into open positions.
  • Stuns or scans give huge control.
  • Movement abilities win close fights.

5. Know When to Disengage

Winning isn’t about fighting everyone. Back off if:

  • You’re third-partied
  • You lost armor
  • Enemy has high ground
  • You’re out of resources

6. Manage Inventory Like a Pro

  • Carry 2–3 stacks of healing.
  • Carry 1–2 stacks of ammo for each weapon.
  • Keep 1 slot for mobility or utility.
  • Drop items you don’t need to stay light and effective.

7. Rotate With Intent

  • Rotate along the edges of the zone to avoid ambushes.
  • Or play center zone if your squad is strong.
  • Always rotate early if the circle is far.

8. Stay Calm in Endgame

Final Circle Tips

  • Keep high ground if possible.
  • Let other teams fight first.
  • Third-party the final fight.
  • Use utility before pushing.
  • Don’t overextend—stay behind cover.

1v1 to Win

  • Heal if you can.
  • Force the enemy into the open.
  • Peek from unexpected angles.
  • Don’t rush unless you have a clear advantage.

9. If You Play in a Team

  • Communicate enemy positions.
  • Focus fire the same target.
  • Assign roles (entry, sniper, support).
  • Share resources.

10. Practice Makes You Dangerous

  • Aim training (tracking + flicks).
  • Learn recoil patterns.
  • Watch top players for movement and positioning.
  • Review your deaths to learn mistakes.
